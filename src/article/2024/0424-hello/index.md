The ring $\mathbb{Z}$ extends the set of natural numbers $\mathbb{N}$ with the **additive inverse** $-1$.

The field of rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$ extends the ring $\mathbb{Z}$ with the **multiplicative inverse** $\frac{1}{n}$ except for the value $0$.

A real number $r \in \mathbb{R}$ is a number that can be used to measure a continuous one-dimensional quantity (such as a distance, duration or temperature). For example, $\sqrt{2}$ measures the distance in a two-dimensional Euclidean space between points $(1,1)$ and $(0,0)$ and is not a rational number.
